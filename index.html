    ESENGO

    Culture Producer & Event Organizer | Sound Artist

    About

    Jeremy Ndoum, " (er/he/il), is a Cultural Producer, Event Organizer and Sound Artist based between the crossroads of Geneva and Basel in Switzerland, which makes him a very forward-thinking artist. His works and participations are focused on creating cultural events that have community reach, as well as educational impact for mainstream public, with a strong sensitivity to social and political components.

    Through his role as a cultural worker he creates events that foster community exchange and sound as a sociopolitical vector of change. At the core of his practice, he believes that access to education and culture is primordial for marginalized communities too.

    As a sound artist, Esengo draws from his African heritage as the roots of his inspiration, traveling through the different Black and queer diasporas, weaving together a rich sonic and historical tapestry. He nurtures these bonds and questions the similarities and differences of these communities through different artistic collaborations, such as in the short movie "NVLH" by Thaís Iroko and Felipe Dutra during their residency at labcinemaexpandido, presenting the archetypal figure of Dona Maria Navalha from Brazilian popular philosophy, which developed in the context of the African diaspora in the region of Rio de Janeiro known as Little Africa, the main port of entry for enslaved Africans in Brazil and the Americas.

    From Motel Campo as a regular here in Geneva, La Créole in Paris, OXI in Berlin, or balls all around Europe, to the prestigious Montreux Jazz Festival, Esengo cemented his name.

    In 2023, Esengo released an edit of a track by UK producer Arma, titled Arma - Sequencing (Esengo Edit), and continued to release his music productions – including a remix for Swiss music producer Fels and a release on TIG WERK (alongside Kaval, King Doudou and more) in support of Légal Team Antiraciste – which reintroduced us to his new role as a music producer.

    Committed to the roots of nightlife and creating reciprocal spaces of joy, Esengo is a member of the club nights collective Angels in Disguise. In June 2024, he released his debut EP, "A KII", paying tribute to dance floors and his ballroom legacy. His work has garnered attention from NYC rapper Cakes da Killa and Nídia, for whom he has opened as a DJ.
