    ESENGO

    Culture Producer & Event Organizer | Sound Artist

    Jeremy Ndoum, known as ESENGO (er/he/il), is a cultural producer, event organizer, and sound artist based between Geneva and Basel, Switzerland.

    As a cultural producer and event organizer, ESENGO develops cultural and educational projects with strong public outreach, grounded in community exchange and political awareness. His work centers access to culture and education for marginalized communities, using sound and nightlife as tools for collective learning and social transformation.

    As a sound artist and DJ, ESENGO draws from his African heritage and the Black and queer diasporas, weaving sonic and historical narratives across club spaces, performances, and artistic collaborations.

    A key work within his artistic practice is the sound design for the short film NVLH by Thaís Iroko and Felipe Dutra, developed during their residency at labcinemaexpandido and screened at the Cinemateca do Museu de Arte Moderna do Rio de Janeiro (MAM).

    His work and performances span venues and contexts such as Motel Campo (Geneva), La Créole (Paris), OXI (Berlin), ballroom events across Europe, and the Montreux Jazz Festival.

    In 2023, he released Arma – Sequencing (ESENGO Edit), followed by productions and remixes for artists including Fels, and a release on TIG WERK in support of Légal Team Antiraciste.

    Committed to the roots of nightlife, ESENGO is a member of the collective Angels in Disguise. In June 2024, he released his debut EP, A KII, paying tribute to dance floors and his ballroom legacy.

    His latest work includes a contribution to Brazilian artist Wesout’s remix project with a remix of Wesout – Pista, mixed and mastered by Brazilian sound engineer Vindit. His work has also been recognized by artists such as Cakes da Killa and Nídia, whom he has supported as a DJ.
